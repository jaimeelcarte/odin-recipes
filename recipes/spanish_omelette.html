Spanish Omelette

Description

It is customary in many cities to celebrate the day of their patron saint with a picnic. In Madrid, on the day of San Isidro, people usually go to the meadow named after the saint and have a picnic lunch of breaded steaks, fried green peppers and above all the classic potato omelet. To make something special I have prepared a potato omelette with confit potatoes and caramelized onion for San Isidro's day, looking for an intense flavor and a particularly creamy texture.

To finish off the process, if you go to the verbena you can prepare or buy some rosquillas de San Isidro, the "tontas" or the "listas", depending on whether you want them with or without a sugar bath and top it off with some sugar and brandy. Of course, if you prepare this tortilla, wherever you are from, you will never forget its flavor in your life.

Ingredients

4 servings:

  • 5 Onions
  • 5 Potatoes
  • 6 Eggs
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • Salt

Steps

  1. To make the recipe there are two tasks: on the one hand to confit the onion in its own sugars, over very low heat and without adding sugar or Modena vinegar, soy sauce or any other ingredient. On the other hand, confit the potatoes by frying them at low temperature so that they are really tender.
  2. To confit the onion, chop it into julienne strips and put it over low heat in a large frying pan with plenty of olive oil. Add a pinch of salt and let it cook slowly. During the first few minutes we will be stirring a lot so that the onion strands are cooked equally.
  3. When the volume of the onion decreases, we can remove part of the oil and let the onion cook until it is reduced to a volume similar to that of a tangerine. Imagine how much it has to be reduced. The process, stirring from time to time, takes about 1 hour.
  4. During this time, peel the potatoes and cut them into thin slices. As we are also going to confit them, we put a frying pan with a lot of oil and add the potatoes to the oil before turning on the fire. When they start frying from the cold oil, they will be super tender. Then we fry them slowly, without cooking the oil at any time.
  5. When the potatoes are ready, beat the eggs in a bowl, add the well-drained potatoes and add the caramelized onion on top. Mix the ingredients well and cook the omelet to your liking. I usually prefer them juicy to eat at home but when they are to eat out like today, we curdle them a little more for safety.
